In order to predict such deformations accurately, one makes use of rod theories. One such rod theory is the one proposed by Simo - it is geometrically exact! This means that the theory makes no assumptions about the deformation of the rod. The deformation can be arbitrarily large and in 3D. The more conventional ~~rod~~ beam theories such are Euler-Bernoulli or Timoshenko are valid only for small, 2D deformations and thus are geometrically inexact!
